  1. Purpose

This SOP outlines the standardized process for campaign setup, ensuring a structured approach to initiating new campaigns or updating existing ones. It defines the request, evaluation, approval, and implementation process to streamline campaign management and optimize telecalling operations.

  1. Scope

This process applies to teams responsible for requesting, evaluating, and implementing campaigns. It ensures that all new campaign requests are assessed based on objectives, feasibility, and alignment with business requirements.

  1. Process Flow

  1. Campaign Request Submission

  1. Ensure a request for a new campaign is submitted via a Google Form by City leads or central Recruitment/performance teams.
  2. The request form includes the following details:
  1. Objective – Justification for the new campaign.
  2. Campaign Type – Inbound or Outbound.
  3. Expected Leads Per Week – Volume estimation.
  4. Telecaller Requirement – Number of TCs needed.
  5. Campaign Expiry Period – 1 month, 6 months, or lifelong.
  1. The request is submitted to the Tech Enabler Team for evaluation.

  1. Campaign Evaluation & Approval Process

  1. The Tech Enabler Team reviews the campaign request.
  2. Decision Points Considered:
  1. Business justification and alignment with existing campaigns.
  2. Feasibility based on system capacity and resource availability.
  3. Redundancy check against existing campaigns.[a]
  1. If the request is approved, ensure a new campaign is created by the tech enabler team and follows the Campaign Management Process.

  2. Campaign Request Rejection

  1. If the request is rejected, the requestor is informed along with the reason for rejection.
  2. The requestor has two options:
  1. If the requester agrees to the reason of rejection then the requester should ensure to use the existing campaign by adding new lead IDs.
  2. If they disagree with the reason for rejection, and the tech enabler is aligning with their request then they  escalate the request to L2 for further review.

  1. Escalation & Final Decision

  1. If the requestor disagrees with the rejection, the matter is escalated to L2 for further discussion.
  2. L2 makes the final decision after analysing the request based on feasibility and necessity:
  1. If the request is still rejected by L2, the requester needs to follow the existing campaign and must be used by adding new lead IDs.
  2. If L2 approves the request after escalation, then the tech enabler creates the campaign and proceeds to the Campaign Management Process.
